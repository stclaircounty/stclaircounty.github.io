## Reform Checklist for St. Clair County

Use this checklist to track progress on needed reforms. Share it with county commissioners and demand accountability.

---

### Essential Reforms

#### Conflict of Interest

- [ ] **Mandatory recusal policy** — Written policy requiring automatic recusal when family members or close personal relationships are involved
- [ ] **Definition of conflicts** — Clear definitions of what constitutes a conflict of interest
- [ ] **Disclosure requirements** — Mandatory disclosure of relationships that could affect impartiality
- [ ] **Enforcement mechanism** — Consequences for failure to recuse

#### Independent Investigations

- [ ] **External agency requirement** — Automatic referral to external agency (State Police, neighboring county) for any investigation involving command staff or their family
- [ ] **Independent investigator pool** — Pre-established agreements with external agencies
- [ ] **No internal handling** — Prohibition on internal investigation of officers connected to command
- [ ] **Public reporting** — Transparent reporting of investigation outcomes

#### Evidence Retention

- [ ] **No personal devices for public business** — Ban on using personal phones/devices for official communications, OR mandatory archiving of all such communications
- [ ] **Automatic retention** — All communications automatically preserved
- [ ] **Litigation hold procedures** — Clear triggers for when holds must be implemented
- [ ] **Penalties for deletion** — Consequences for destroying public records

#### Oversight & Accountability

- [ ] **County commissioner oversight** — Real authority to review sheriff operations
- [ ] **Regular audits** — Periodic review of policies and compliance
- [ ] **Public reporting requirements** — Transparency in operations
- [ ] **Citizen complaint process** — Clear, accessible process for filing complaints

---

### How to Use This Checklist

1. **Attend county commissioner meetings** — Bring this checklist and ask about each item
2. **Request updates** — Ask for written status on each reform
3. **Document responses** — Keep records of what officials say
4. **Share publicly** — Post updates on social media, write letters to editors
5. **Vote** — Hold elected officials accountable at the ballot box

---

### Questions to Ask Officials

- "Has the county adopted a mandatory recusal policy for the Sheriff's Office?"
- "What happens when an investigation involves the Sheriff's family member?"
- "Are text messages on personal phones being preserved?"
- "Has a litigation hold ever been issued? When?"
- "Who has oversight authority over the Sheriff's Office?"
