## Draft County Policy Ordinance

This draft ordinance addresses the governance failures documented on this site. It can be presented to the St. Clair County Board of Commissioners for consideration.

---

### ORDINANCE NO. ____

**AN ORDINANCE TO ESTABLISH ACCOUNTABILITY AND TRANSPARENCY REQUIREMENTS FOR THE ST. CLAIR COUNTY SHERIFF'S OFFICE**

---

#### SECTION 1. PURPOSE

The purpose of this ordinance is to:

(a) Ensure public trust in law enforcement through transparent and accountable governance;

(b) Require mandatory recusal when conflicts of interest exist;

(c) Establish evidence retention and preservation requirements;

(d) Require independent investigation of matters involving command staff;

(e) Provide meaningful oversight authority to the County Board of Commissioners.

---

#### SECTION 2. DEFINITIONS

(a) **"Conflict of interest"** means any situation where a decision-maker has a personal, familial, or financial relationship with the subject of an investigation or decision that could reasonably be perceived to affect impartiality.

(b) **"Family member"** means spouse, parent, child, sibling, grandparent, grandchild, aunt, uncle, niece, nephew, or cousin, whether by blood, marriage, or adoption.

(c) **"Close personal relationship"** means a friendship or association beyond professional duties that could reasonably be perceived to affect impartiality.

(d) **"Command staff"** means the Sheriff, Undersheriff, Captains, and Lieutenants.

---

#### SECTION 3. MANDATORY RECUSAL

(a) **Automatic Recusal Required.** Any Sheriff's Office employee shall automatically recuse themselves from any investigation, decision, or official action when:

   (1) The subject is a family member;

   (2) The subject is a close personal friend;

   (3) The employee has any financial interest in the outcome;

   (4) Any other circumstance exists that would cause a reasonable person to question the employee's impartiality.

(b) **Documentation.** All recusals shall be documented in writing and reported to the County Administrator within 24 hours.

(c) **Prohibition on Participation.** Recused individuals shall have no involvement in the matter, including informal discussions, advice, or access to investigation materials.

---

#### SECTION 4. INDEPENDENT INVESTIGATION REQUIREMENT

(a) **External Agency Referral.** Any investigation involving:

   (1) The Sheriff or immediate family member;

   (2) Command staff or immediate family member;

   (3) Allegations of criminal conduct by any Sheriff's Office employee;

   Shall be automatically referred to an external law enforcement agency for investigation.

(b) **Pre-Established Agreements.** The Sheriff's Office shall maintain current memoranda of understanding with at least two external agencies for such investigations.

(c) **No Internal Handling.** Matters subject to this section shall not be investigated internally under any circumstances.

---

#### SECTION 5. EVIDENCE RETENTION AND PRESERVATION

(a) **Electronic Communications.** All electronic communications related to official business, regardless of device (work-issued or personal), shall be:

   (1) Preserved for a minimum of seven (7) years;

   (2) Subject to FOIA requests;

   (3) Backed up automatically where technically feasible.

(b) **Litigation Hold.** When any of the following occurs, a litigation hold shall be immediately implemented:

   (1) Arrest of any Sheriff's Office employee;

   (2) Filing of any lawsuit against the Sheriff's Office or employee;

   (3) Receipt of any claim or notice of intent to sue;

   (4) Any incident likely to result in litigation.

(c) **Prohibition on Destruction.** No employee shall delete, destroy, or alter any record that is or may be relevant to any investigation or legal matter.

(d) **Penalties.** Violation of this section shall result in disciplinary action up to and including termination, and may be referred for criminal prosecution.

---

#### SECTION 6. OVERSIGHT AND REPORTING

(a) **Quarterly Reports.** The Sheriff shall provide quarterly reports to the Board of Commissioners including:

   (1) All internal investigations initiated and their outcomes;

   (2) All matters referred to external agencies;

   (3) All recusals documented;

   (4) All litigation holds in effect.

(b) **Annual Audit.** The County shall conduct an annual audit of compliance with this ordinance.

(c) **Public Access.** Reports required by this section shall be public records.

---

#### SECTION 7. ENFORCEMENT

(a) The County Administrator shall monitor compliance with this ordinance.

(b) Violations shall be reported to the Board of Commissioners.

(c) The Board of Commissioners may take such action as authorized by law.

---

#### SECTION 8. EFFECTIVE DATE

This ordinance shall take effect 60 days after adoption.
