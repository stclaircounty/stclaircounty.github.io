## Remediation Plan

This plan outlines specific actions needed to address the systemic governance failures documented on this site.

---

### Phase 1: Immediate Actions

#### 1.1 Mandatory Recusal Policy

**Action:** Adopt written policy requiring automatic recusal when:
- Investigation involves family member (any degree of relation)
- Investigation involves close personal friend
- Any relationship that could create appearance of bias

**Implementation:**
- Draft policy within 30 days
- Public comment period
- Adoption by county commission
- Training for all personnel

#### 1.2 External Investigation Requirement

**Action:** Establish formal agreements with external agencies for independent investigations.

**Implementation:**
- Memorandum of Understanding with Michigan State Police
- Mutual aid agreements with neighboring counties
- Clear triggers for automatic external referral
- No exceptions for "minor" matters involving command staff

#### 1.3 Litigation Hold Procedures

**Action:** Implement automatic litigation hold when legal action is foreseeable.

**Implementation:**
- Written procedures for initiating holds
- Automatic preservation of all electronic communications
- Regular compliance audits
- Penalties for non-compliance

---

### Phase 2: Structural Reforms

#### 2.1 Digital Evidence Governance

**Action:** Comprehensive policy for electronic communications.

**Requirements:**
- No personal devices for public business, OR
- Mandatory archiving of all communications on personal devices
- Automatic backup systems
- Regular audits of compliance

#### 2.2 Oversight Authority

**Action:** Establish meaningful county oversight of sheriff operations.

**Requirements:**
- Regular reporting to county commission
- Authority to request records and explanations
- Public meetings with opportunity for citizen input
- Annual compliance review

#### 2.3 Public Reporting

**Action:** Transparency in investigations and outcomes.

**Requirements:**
- Public reporting of all internal investigation outcomes
- Statistical reporting on complaints and dispositions
- Annual transparency report
- Accessible records request process

---

### Phase 3: Accountability

#### 3.1 Review of Past Decisions

**Action:** Independent review of decisions made by conflicted parties.

**Scope:**
- All decisions regarding November 2022 OWI matter
- Any other matters where conflicts existed
- Assessment of whether policies were violated

#### 3.2 Investigation of Evidence Destruction

**Action:** Refer evidence spoliation to appropriate authorities.

**Options:**
- Michigan Attorney General Public Integrity Unit
- County prosecutor (if independent)
- DOJ Civil Rights Division

#### 3.3 Personnel Accountability

**Action:** Appropriate consequences for policy violations.

**Considerations:**
- Due process for all personnel
- Proportionate consequences
- Focus on systemic reform, not just individual punishment
- Prevention of future violations
