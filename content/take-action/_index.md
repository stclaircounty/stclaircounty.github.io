## Make Your Voice Heard

Government accountability requires engaged citizens. Here's how you can help bring transparency to St. Clair County.

## Contact Your Representatives

### St. Clair County Board of Commissioners

The Board of Commissioners provides oversight of county departments and services.

- Request independent investigations into misconduct
- Demand policies requiring recusal in conflict of interest situations
- Ask for regular public reporting on internal affairs matters
- Push for stronger FOIA compliance

### Michigan Attorney General

For matters involving potential criminal conduct by government officials.

### Michigan State Police

Request investigation by an independent agency when local oversight fails.

### Judicial Tenure Commission

For complaints regarding judicial conduct.

## Public Records Requests

You have the right to access public records under the Michigan Freedom of Information Act (FOIA).

**What to request:**
- Internal affairs policies and procedures
- Recusal policies for conflicts of interest
- Communication retention policies
- Training records
- Meeting minutes and agendas
- Contracts and expenditures

**Tips for effective FOIA requests:**
- Be specific about what you're requesting
- Include date ranges when applicable
- Request electronic copies to reduce fees
- Follow up if you don't receive a response within 5 business days

## Attend Public Meetings

Public meetings are where government decisions are made — your presence matters.

- **County Commission meetings** — budget, oversight, and policy decisions
- **Public safety committee meetings** — law enforcement policies
- **Court proceedings** — open to the public in most cases
- **School board meetings** — education governance
