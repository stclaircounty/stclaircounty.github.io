## Deposition Overview

This deposition contains sworn testimony from Captain Matthew Pohl regarding his involvement in the aftermath of Deputy Marc King's November 2022 OWI arrest, including his admission to deleting relevant text messages.

## Key Issues Addressed

### Personal Relationship

Captain Pohl acknowledged his close friendship with Deputy Marc King:

- Long-standing personal relationship
- Social connections outside of work
- The relationship's potential impact on his objectivity

### Communication with Sheriff King

The deposition covers Captain Pohl's communications with Sheriff Mat King:

- Being contacted on the day of the arrest
- Discussions about how to handle the matter
- His role in the internal handling of the incident

### Evidence Deletion

**Critical testimony regarding deleted evidence:**

Captain Pohl admitted under oath to deleting text messages from the time period surrounding the November 2022 incident:

- Messages were deleted despite their potential relevance
- No litigation hold was in place
- No effort was made to preserve the communications
- The content of the deleted messages is unknown

## Legal Significance

Captain Pohl's admission to deleting evidence has significant legal implications:

### Adverse Inference

Under the spoliation doctrine, when evidence is destroyed, courts may instruct juries to assume the destroyed evidence would have been unfavorable to the party that destroyed it.

### Discovery Sanctions

In ongoing litigation, the deletion of relevant evidence can result in sanctions including:

- Monetary penalties
- Adverse jury instructions
- Default judgments in extreme cases

### Criminal Exposure

Under Michigan law (MCL 750.491), evidence tampering can constitute a criminal offense.
