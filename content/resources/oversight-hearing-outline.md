## Oversight Hearing Outline

Structured questions for county commissioners conducting oversight of the St. Clair County Sheriff's Office.

---

### Purpose

This outline provides a framework for county commissioners to conduct meaningful oversight regarding the governance failures documented on this site.

---

### Opening Statement

> "The purpose of this hearing is to understand what happened, why our existing systems failed to prevent it, and what changes are needed to ensure public trust in the Sheriff's Office. This is not a trial or disciplinary proceeding. It is an oversight function to inform policy decisions."

---

### Section 1: Conflict of Interest

#### Questions for Sheriff King

1. "Sheriff King, is Deputy Marc King your brother?"

2. "Were you notified of his arrest on November 6, 2022?"

3. "Did you recuse yourself from any involvement in the matter?"
   - If no: "Why not?"
   - If yes: "Please describe the scope of your recusal."

4. "Does the Sheriff's Office have a written policy requiring recusal when family members are involved?"
   - If yes: "Please provide a copy."
   - If no: "Why not?"

5. "Who made the decision not to refer this matter to an external agency?"

6. "Do you believe it is appropriate for a Sheriff to be involved in any capacity in an investigation of their own sibling?"

---

### Section 2: Investigation Handling

#### Questions for Sheriff King

1. "Who was assigned to handle the internal review of this matter?"

2. "Was that individual aware of the family relationship?"

3. "Were any individuals assigned to this matter who had personal relationships with Deputy Marc King?"

4. "What was the outcome of any internal investigation?"

5. "Why was no external agency — such as Michigan State Police — engaged to conduct an independent investigation?"

6. "Does the Sheriff's Office have any agreements with external agencies for independent investigations?"

---

### Section 3: Evidence Preservation

#### Questions for Sheriff King

1. "When a Sheriff's Office employee is arrested, is a litigation hold automatically implemented?"
   - If yes: "Was one implemented in this case?"
   - If no: "Why not?"

2. "Are you aware that Captain Pohl testified under oath that he deleted text messages related to this matter?"

3. "Are you aware that Deputy Duva testified that text messages are 'no longer available'?"

4. "What is the Sheriff's Office policy on retention of text messages and electronic communications?"

5. "Are employees permitted to use personal devices for official business?"
   - If yes: "Are those communications preserved?"

6. "What action, if any, has been taken regarding the destruction of evidence?"

#### Questions for Captain Pohl (if present)

1. "Captain Pohl, did you delete text messages related to the November 2022 arrest of Deputy Marc King?"

2. "When did you delete these messages?"

3. "Why did you delete these messages?"

4. "Were you aware at the time that these messages might be relevant to legal proceedings?"

5. "Did anyone direct you to delete these messages?"

---

### Section 4: Policy Gaps

#### Questions for Sheriff King

1. "What written policies govern conflicts of interest in the Sheriff's Office?"

2. "What written policies govern evidence retention?"

3. "What written policies govern when external investigations are required?"

4. "Will you commit to adopting mandatory recusal policies?"

5. "Will you commit to adopting evidence retention policies that prevent deletion of relevant communications?"

6. "Will you commit to establishing agreements with external agencies for independent investigations?"

---

### Section 5: Oversight Authority

#### Questions for County Administrator/Counsel

1. "What oversight authority does the County Commission have over the Sheriff's Office?"

2. "Can the County Commission require the Sheriff's Office to adopt specific policies?"

3. "What recourse does the County have if the Sheriff's Office fails to implement recommended reforms?"

4. "Are there model policies from other Michigan counties that could be adopted here?"

---

### Closing Questions

1. "Sheriff King, if faced with a similar situation today, would you do anything differently?"

2. "What assurance can you provide to county residents that this will not happen again?"

3. "Are you willing to work with the County Commission on adopting reforms?"

---

### Follow-Up Actions

After the hearing, commissioners should:

1. **Document findings** — Written summary of testimony received
2. **Request records** — Follow up on any documents referenced
3. **Draft policies** — Prepare ordinances addressing gaps
4. **Set timeline** — Establish deadlines for reform implementation
5. **Schedule follow-up** — Plan subsequent oversight hearings
6. **Public report** — Issue public findings and recommendations

---

### Legal Considerations

- Commissioners should consult with county counsel regarding scope of authority
- Witnesses may have Fifth Amendment rights
- Focus on policy reform, not disciplinary action (different processes)
- Maintain public record of proceedings
