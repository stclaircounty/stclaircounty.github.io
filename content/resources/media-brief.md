## Media Quick Brief

For journalists and media outlets covering the St. Clair County Sheriff's Office accountability story.

---

### Story Focus

**Process, not personalities.**

This story is about systemic governance failures, not individual misconduct. The focus should be on:

- Conflict of interest policies (or lack thereof)
- Evidence preservation failures
- Independent investigation requirements
- Oversight mechanisms

---

### Headline Framing

**Recommended:**
> "County residents seek independent oversight after evidence gaps emerge"

> "Governance questions raised after Sheriff investigated brother's arrest"

> "Deleted texts, no recusal: St. Clair County accountability failures documented"

**Avoid:**
- Sensationalized framing
- Personal accusations without evidence
- Political characterizations

### Key Questions for Officials

**For Sheriff King:**
- Why did you not recuse yourself?
- Why was no external agency engaged?
- What is the department's recusal policy?
- Has a litigation hold ever been implemented?

**For County Commissioners:**
- What oversight authority exists over the Sheriff's Office?
- Will the county adopt mandatory recusal policies?
- What action will be taken regarding deleted evidence?

**For Captain Pohl/Deputy Duva:**
- What was in the deleted messages?
- Why were messages deleted?
- Were you directed to delete messages?

---

### Context

**What this is about:**

A deputy was arrested for drunk driving. His brother, the Sheriff, handled the matter internally without recusing himself. Multiple officers deleted relevant text messages. No policies required recusal or independent investigation.

**What this is NOT about:**

- The underlying OWI arrest itself
- Whether the deputy was guilty
- Political opposition to any official
- Personal grievances

---

### Residents' Concerns

1. Equal treatment under the law
2. Independent investigation of officer misconduct
3. Preservation of public records
4. Meaningful oversight of sheriff operations

---

### Proposed Reforms

Residents are calling for:

- Mandatory recusal policies
- External investigation requirements for command staff matters
- Digital evidence retention requirements
- County oversight authority
