What You Can Send Us

Documents & Evidence

Court filings, FOIA responses, official correspondence, internal memos, photographs, videos, or any documentation related to accountability issues.

Tips & Information

Firsthand accounts, leads on developing stories, information about incidents not yet documented, or context that helps us understand existing cases.

Corrections

If you find any errors, outdated information, or inaccuracies on this site, please let us know. We take accuracy seriously and correct verified errors promptly.

General Inquiries

Questions about the project, media inquiries, collaboration requests, or any other correspondence related to our mission.

Source Protection

We understand that sharing information about government misconduct can be sensitive. We take source protection seriously and will maintain confidentiality when requested.

  • Anonymous submissions are accepted
  • We never reveal sources without explicit permission
  • Documents are verified independently before publication

Before You Send

  • 1 Verify authenticity. We only publish documents we can verify. Include information about the source and how you obtained the materials.
  • 2 Provide context. Help us understand what the documents show and why they matter. The more context, the faster we can review.
  • 3 Indicate confidentiality needs. Let us know if you need to remain anonymous or if there are any restrictions on how the information can be used.
  • 4 Be patient. We review all submissions carefully. Complex materials may take time to verify and publish.

Response Times

We aim to acknowledge all emails within 48 hours. Document review and verification may take longer depending on complexity. If your matter is time-sensitive, please indicate that in your subject line.
